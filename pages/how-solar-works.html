How Solar Works

Understanding the science and benefits of solar energy

The Science Behind Solar Energy

Solar energy is one of the most abundant and cleanest sources of renewable energy available today. Understanding how solar panels work can help you make an informed decision about going solar.

Solar panels convert sunlight directly into electricity using photovoltaic (PV) cells. These cells are made from semiconductor materials, typically silicon, that absorb photons from sunlight and release electrons, creating an electric current.

How Solar Energy Works

From sunlight to electricity in your home

1

Sunlight Hits Solar Panels

Photovoltaic cells in solar panels absorb photons from sunlight, creating an electric field across the layers.

2

DC Electricity is Generated

When photons hit the solar cells, they knock electrons loose, creating direct current (DC) electricity.

3

Inverter Converts to AC

The inverter converts DC electricity to alternating current (AC) that your home appliances can use.

4

Power Your Home

AC electricity flows through your electrical panel to power lights, appliances, and other devices.

5

Excess Energy to Grid

Any excess electricity is sent back to the grid, earning you credits on your utility bill.

6

Monitor Your System

Track your energy production and savings with our monitoring system and mobile app.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about how solar energy works

How much sunlight do I need for solar panels?

Solar panels work in most climates, but they're most effective in areas with consistent sunlight. Even in cloudy weather, panels can still generate electricity, though at reduced efficiency.

How long do solar panels last?

Most solar panels come with a 5-year warranty and can last 30+ years with proper maintenance. They typically lose about 0.5% efficiency per year.

Do solar panels work at night?

Solar panels don't generate electricity at night, but you can use battery storage to store excess energy during the day for use at night, or rely on the grid.

What happens during a power outage?

Without battery storage, solar panels automatically shut off during outages for safety. With battery storage, you can continue to power essential appliances.
